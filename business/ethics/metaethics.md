### what is metaethics?
- fundamental nature of moral values, judgements, and language
- "*what* is morality?" rather than "right or wrong"
- Are moral claims facts, opinions, feelings, or social rules?

> What does 'wrong' even mean

----
### moral objectivism (moral realism)
- there are moral facts
- moral claims can be true or false
- these facts are independent of what people think
- "stealing is wrong even if people disagree"

### 1. metaphysical worry
- where is rightness or goodness in the world?
### 2. knowledge worry
- if moral facts are weird?
	- how we know them?
		- Intuition, Feelings, Perception
### 3. motivational worry
- "***what*** motivates you to return your friend's wallet"
### 4. diversity worry
- there isn't objective fact of the matter about morality
- different cultures have different morals
	- no objective truth

----

### anti-realist responses (no objective morality)
- **moral nihilism** 
	- here are no moral facts
	- nothing is really right or w

1. error theory
	- no moral fact exist
	- all moral judgments are false
	- no moral knowledge
2. expressivism (non-cognitivism)
	- moral statements don't describe facts
	- they express emotions or commands

- **cultural relativism**
	- an action is right if and only if a society approves of it
- **subjectivism**
	- an action is right if and only if I approve of it
- **ideal observer theory**
	- an act is right if a fully informed, perfectly rational person would approve of it

---
### normative ethics (what should we do)
- utilitarianism (consequences matter)
	- an action is right if it maximizes happiness / utility
	- consequences determine morality
	- ACT - judge each action individually
	- RULE - follow rules that maximize utility 

---

### kantian deontology
- some actions are wrong no matter the consequences
- treat people as ends never as mere means

---

### virtue ethics
- what would a virtuous person do in this situation

---

### SUMMARY
metaethics = what morality is
realism = moral facts exist
relativism = morality depends on culture
subjectivism = morality depends on individual
utilitarianism = maximize happiness
kant = duty & respect
virtue ethics = be a good person
