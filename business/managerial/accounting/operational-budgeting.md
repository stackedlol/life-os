### what is an operational budget?
- annual budget: is a detailed financial plan for a company's upcoming year
	- sales projection
	- cost estimates
	- target profit

1. Sales Budget & Expected Cash Collections
	- first step of the budgeting process
	- predicts sales revenue for each period
	- helps estimate cash inflows from customer payments

2. production or inventory purchases budget & expected cash payments
	- determines how many units to product or but to meet sales demand:
		- inventory goals
		- timing of purchases
		- cash outflows for materials or stock

3. Labor, Overhead, and Selling/Admin Expense budgets
	- labor budget - estimates total wages based on hours needed
	- overhead budget - includes all other production costs (utilities, maintenance)
	- selling & administrative budget - covers office expenses, marketing, and etc

4. Cash Budget
	- tracks cash inflows (money in) and cash outflows (money out)
	- shows if the company will have cash to cover expenses

5. budgeted income statement 
	- estimates expected profit or loss for the year
	- combines all previous budgets

6. budgeted balance sheet
	- predicts the company's financial position at year-end
	- shows expected assets, liabilities, and equity
