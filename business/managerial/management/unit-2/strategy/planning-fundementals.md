### the mission statement
- **mission** - is its purpose or reason for being
	- who we are and what we do
	- determined by top management and board
- **mission statement** - which expresses the purpose of the organization

### the vision statement
- is a long-term goal describing "what" an organization wants to become
- developed after the mission statement

	- Clarity - employees understand the vision statement
	- Future Focus - the vision statement describes the future, not the current state
	- abstractness and challenge - the future described as hypothetical and difficult, but achievable
	- idealism - the future is portrayed as being highly desirable

### the values statement
- expresses what the company stands for, core priorities, the values its employees embody, and what its products contribute to the world 


### three types of planning
1. **strategic** - is a process that determines what the organization's long-term goals should be for the next one to five years with the resources they expect to have available

2. **tactical** - determine what contributions their departments or similar work units can make with their given resources during the next 6-24 months

3. **operational** - determine how to accomplish specific tasks with available resources within the next 1-52 weeks
